01 — About

I'm a PhD researcher in Computer Science at George Mason University, where I build perception and planning systems for autonomous robots. My work lives at the intersection of 3D computer vision, multi-sensor fusion, and embodied AI — systems that not only see the world but reason about it.

At the Living Innovation Lab, I develop real-time pipelines fusing LiDAR, RGB-T cameras, and GNSS on edge hardware for warehouse robots — from change detection to 6DOF end-effector pose estimation for robotic arms. My research has been deployed on actual hardware, not just benchmarks.

My CoRL 2024 workshop paper introduced an LLM-augmented planning approach for robot object search — using language model predictions to bias navigation toward semantically plausible locations, reducing search steps significantly.

Before GMU, I published Q1 research at BRAC University and shipped embedded ML systems on Jetson Nano for real-time vehicle perception at Arthor Limited. I placed 3rd and 4th at the University Rover Challenge — one of the few competitions where the robot has to survive in the real world, not just a simulation.

Custom conditional DDPM trained on KITTI-style data for depth completion. Implements a linear beta schedule (1000 timesteps, Ho et al. 2020) with a conditioning network that takes semantic labels and plane distance as priors.
